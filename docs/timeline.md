## How to read the timeline

The colonists timestamp their logs two ways:

- **Days** — "day 620", "day 758". The in-fiction clock of the doomed Coral Gardens colony. [`DA_DataBankEntry_Kurultai1`; `DA_DataBankEntry_Kurultai3`; `DA_CoralGardens_Twinkle_Response_Audiolog_Dialogue`]
- **Hours ago** — blackbox postmortems say e.g. "died about 235,000 hours ago." 235,000 h ≈ **26.8 years**. That's where the player sits in the timeline. [`DA_CoralGardens_BlackBox_Chap_02_DatabankEntry`; `DA_Ruins_BlackBox_Iso_01_DatabankEntry`; `DA_Ruins_BlackBox_Singh_03_DatabankEntry`; etc.]

## I. The Far Background — Human Civilization Before the Cicada

### 21st–22nd century: Earth's Holocene Collapse and the Expansion

Earth's biosphere collapses. Humanity scatters into a bloody, accelerated colonization race called the **Expansion**. The **Alterra Corporation** corners the arms market during this period, then embargoes its own customers to force peace. The resulting settlement produces **the Charter** and a new rules-based interstellar order — the *transgovs*. [`DA_Alterra`; `DA_DataBankEntry_Alterra`]

Alterra's motto, *"Get What You Deserve,"* defines the era. Ethical action is "the rational pursuit of joint profit." [`DA_Alterra`]

### 2158: The Mongolian Independent States break away

A Mongolian-themed transgov splits off from the Chinese Territories and pushes into the **Ariadne Arm** with one-way, slower-than-light ships. Citizenship is not ethnically restricted — anyone who adopts the culture is welcome. Pay-rate names like *Nökhör* (companion), *Bagatur* (hero), and *Kurul* (council member) survive into the Cicada manifest as marks of status. [`DA_MongolianIndependentStates_DatabankEntry`; `DA_DataBankEntry_Mongolians`]; pay-rate annotations on every colonist biography, e.g. [`DA_CoralGardens_BlackBox_Bio_Mantis_DatabankEntry`; `DA_CoralGardens_BlackBox_Bio_Anita_DatabankEntry`; `DA_Personnel_Zip`]

### Late 22nd century: 4546b and the first kharaa contact

Alterra discovers a dangerous spaceborne bacterium on the water world **4546b** and conceals it, cooperating with the **TSF** to seize the surrounding territory from the Mongolian Independent States. [`DA_Alterra`; `DA_Kharaa`]

This is the world of *Subnautica 1*. **Ryley Robinson** escapes and writes a memoir, *4546B: SURVIVING THE IMPOSSIBLE*. Reaper-leviathan statuettes are still sold as furniture 200 years later. [`DA_BasePiece_ReaperStatue_DatabankEntry1`]

The Sophie simulation in the Tadpole Pens NoA goes further: *"Ryley Robinson found more than kharaa on 4546b. Leviathan incubators. Anti-orbital guns. A genuine doomsday device."* [`DA_CoralGardens_Sophie_ProtectedZone_Audiolog_Dialogue`; `DA_DataBankEntry_Sophie_ProtectedZone`]

Alterra also recovers **alien phase gate technology** from 4546b — the basis of the TSF Frontiersmen's "mini-phasegate" tools. [`DA_DataBankEntry_Nahema2`; `DA_CoralGardens_Nahema2_Audiolog_Dialogue`]

This is also when humanity first contacts the **Architects** (*Almanach instantia*). Information is classified under TransGov Charter 1.c. PDAs are programmed to translate Architect Linear, but colonists are ordered never to approach Architect tech directly. [`DA_DataBankEntry_Architects`]

### Turn of the 23rd century: The orange kharaa and the fall of the Mongolians

A second, more dangerous strain of kharaa — the *orange* or *Sanji* strain — spreads through free space on spores. Survivors call it *kharaal*, "the curse"; the carpet-like growth it produces is *toiruulga*. [`DA_Kharaa`; `DA_CoralGardens_MantisKharaa_Audiolog_Dialogue`; `DA_DataBankEntry_MantisKharaa_QuartzChip`; `DA_Char1_Habitat`]

Mantis voices the recurring suspicion: *"There were rumors Alterra and the TSF let it spread, so they could take over the Steppes."* [`DA_CoralGardens_MantisKharaa_Audiolog_Dialogue`]

The Mongolian Independent States effectively cease to exist. Refugees scatter, and **Alterra sponsors them** — possibly to retain their early-outbreak knowledge. [`DA_Alterra`; `DA_MongolianIndependentStates_DatabankEntry`]

Meanwhile, **Admiral Rathine Studaber** uses the kharaa war to detach the TSF from corporate sponsors. Analysts expect him to declare martial law in a transgov colony imminently. [`DA_TSF`; `DA_DataBankEntry_TSF`]

### The Cicada contract

The deal Alterra offers the Mongolian refugees is brutal:

> *"I, the Pawn, agree: that I will be transported as digital information aboard the colony ship Cicada to the planet Zezura… that I will construct, improve, and operate a colony on the New World without wage or salary, working off my debt, that I refuse holiday, retirement, or permanent death until the Noetic Advisor System ('NoA') determines that a permanent, self-sustaining human future on the New World is achieved."* [`DA_DataBankEntry_YourContract`]

Colonists are stored as **biobed quantum images** — phase-conjugation echoes in optical quartz, not digital data, so each image produces exactly one printed body. The technology is described in detail at [`DA_BasePiece_Biobed_DatabankEntry`; `DA_Death`; `DA_SurvivingDeath`; `DA_DeathAndRecoveryFAQ`; `DA_FAQDeathandRecovery`]

The mission is overseen by **NoA** — the **Noetic Advisor** — Alterra's "weakly supervisable," "frozen" AI, originally built for kharaa quarantine. Architecture: a minimal central executive aboard the *Cicada* (the **ANu-NoA**) controls **limb-nodes** at every worksite, like an octopus. NoA cannot revise its own values. Motto: *"How can I improve you today?"* [`DA_NOA`; `DA_DataBankEntry_Noa`; `DA_BasePiece_NoATerminal_DatabankEntry`]

## II. The Axum — A Civilization That Failed Without Fire

### Singh's reconstruction

The colony xenobiologist's three field-note logs are the spine of what we know about the Axum:

- **Lobster-aliens** — large arthropods with claws, polarized vision, and an electric sense like a shark's. [`DA_Audiolog_TailingsSingh2_DatabankEntry`; `DA_CoralGardens_Tailings_Singh0_Audiolog_Dialogue`; `DA_Wreck_SuperScanner_Blackbox_Iso_Dialogue`]
- **They invented electricity *before* fire.** They felt current in seabed bacterial fibers, bundled the fibers into generators, and used **electrowinning** to pull metal from seawater. *"To us, electricity means modernity. To the Axum, it's as old as bronze."* [`DA_Audiolog_TailingsSingh2_DatabankEntry`; `DA_Ruins_MetalFarming_DatabankEntry`]
- **They never cleared their oceans.** Apex predators that could eat them "like cocktail shrimp" still swim through the Karakorum ruins. [`DA_Audiolog_TailingsSingh3_DatabankEntry`]

The Tailings live in lightweight salvage shelters of glued fish leather and electroformed hatches over an organic-plastic frame. [`DA_CoralGardens_TailingsVillage_DatabankEntry`; `DA_POI_Tailings_Village_DatabankEntry`]

Singh's most devastating note:

> *"What I see here isn't the equivalent of a premodern Earth culture with a sophisticated survival package… The children of a bunch of office workers and day trippers forced to learn wilderness survival from scratch."* [`DA_CoralGardens_Tailings_Singh0_Audiolog_Dialogue`; `DA_DataBankEntry_Tailings2`]

### ~1200 years ago: The Karakorum Observatory

Cut granite blocks, coated in electroformed silicon-bronze, raised above the seabed to lift telescopes near the surface. Newton-style reflecting telescopes built with electroplated silver mirrors. **The Observatory's top no longer breaks the surface** — sea level has risen hundreds of meters since construction. [`DA_Story_Axum_Observatory_Exterior_DataBankEntry`; `DA_Ruins_Observatory_DatabankEntry`; `DA_BP_Observatory_Telescope_DatabankEntry`]

Inside is an **orrery** — a model of the Eido-Thea binary system: two M0V red dwarfs in contact, seven planets, three moons. Proteus is a moon of a gas giant. [`DA_Ruins_Orrery_DatabankEntry`; `DA_Observatory_Orrery_DatabankEntry`]

Scanning the orrery's pulsar lights lets the PDA fix the colony's galactic position: **near PSR K1745-2856, rimward of the globular cluster Djorgovski-2, at the base of the Norma Arm. ~20,000 years from human space. Self-rescue impossible.** [`DA_GalacticPosition_DatabankEntry`]

### The Karakorum Power Plant

A hybrid plant the colonists call **Karakorum**, originally hydroelectric, fed by hot water from a titan rockbore below. [`DA_Ruins_PowerPlant_DatabankEntry`; `DA_POI_Powerplant_DatabankEntry`; `DA_Ruins_PowerPlantNeedle_DatabankEntry`]

- ~300 years ago — retrofitted to burn methane (with huge methane storage tanks added). [`DA_Ruins_PowerPlantComponent1_DatabankEntry`; `DA_Audiolog_KarakorumPower3MethaneTanks_DatabankEntry`; `DA_Ruins_PowerPlant_DatabankEntry`]
- ~250 years ago — sabotaged by thermite. Plant abandoned. [`DA_Ruins_PowerPlantComponent1_DatabankEntry`; `DA_Ruins_PowerPlant_DatabankEntry`]

The Axum knew their methane addiction was wrecking their climate; the **Viceroys** were a body of leaders appointed to manage the crisis. [`DA_AxumRuins_Observatory_InnerSanctum_DatabankEntry`]

### The three factions and the war that broke Xanadu

The Axum politics splinter into three known camps. The audiolog *"Powerplant is Dead"* — written by an Axum survivor — names the Viceroys directly:

> *"The powerplant is dead. Spies set fires in its parts. Relatives say the Viceroys did this. Why? Our claws hurt from work. We cannot fix it. We cannot become a molt which can fix it. Sound says this is happening everywhere. The message cables are cut. Why? Xanadu has gone to war against itself. It is hot without the pumps."* [`DA_Audiolog_KarakorumPower1Axum_DatabankEntry`; `DA_Ruins_KarakorumPowerAxum_Audiolog_Dialogue`]

- **The Viceroys** — appointed managers of the climate crisis; architects of the global sabotage. [`DA_Audiolog_KarakorumPower1Axum_DatabankEntry`; `DA_AxumRuins_Observatory_InnerSanctum_DatabankEntry`]
- **The Crypters** — Axum who chose **hibernation** in vaults below the **World Tree** at the site **Xanadu**. [`DA_ClimateLab_AnalyzingMessage_Dialogue`; `DA_ClimateLab_Architect_Dialogue`; `DA_AxumRuins_Observatory_InnerSanctum_DatabankEntry`; `DA_AxumDataCache_DatabankEntry`] The Crypter Terminal still operates, asking for help locating "misplaced trinkets." [`DA_AxumCrypterTerminal_DatabankEntry`]
- **The Soterists** — a faction whose plan was *alien intervention*. They found an Architect phase gate at the Lagrange point between Proteus and its sister moon **Apiary**, and built **Contact Labs** to broadcast a homing signal for the Architects, near sites of natural and cultural interest — but pointedly *not* at Xanadu, which the Crypters controlled. Some may be booby-trapped. [`DA_AxumRuins_ContactLab_Reveal_DatabankEntry`]

### ~200 years ago: The Climate Lab

A titanium-palladium alloy sphere bolted onto the top of the ancient Observatory, anchored by a tether containing power and access cables. Far more advanced than the tower beneath it — sonar, lidar, magnetometers, plankton sensors, Doppler radar, a plutonium battery. Builders comparable to Earth's Information Age. [`DA_Observatory_ClimateLabExterior_DatabankEntry`; `DA_Observatory_ClimateLabConnector_DatabankEntry`; `DA_Ruins_ClimateLab_DatabankEntry`]

Purpose: measure Proteus' dying climate and signal the Crypters in their vaults when it is either safe to emerge or so bad that "more extreme measures" are required. [`DA_AxumRuins_Observatory_InnerSanctum_DatabankEntry`]

### The Tailings

The Axum survivors who guard the ruins. The colony PDA names them after mining spoil. They camp above the hot caves, use spears made of harvested metal, and kill colonists on sight at the ruins — possibly *teaching* them: cross this line, you die. [`DA_POI_Tailings_Village_DatabankEntry`; `DA_CoralGardens_TailingsVillage_DatabankEntry`; `DA_Audiolog_TailingsSingh1_DatabankEntry`; `DA_CoralGardens_BlackBox_Singh_01_DatabankEntry`]

## III. The Cicada Diversion

The *Cicada* was bound for **Zezura**, fourteen years outside transgov space. None of the colonists remember a crash. [`DA_CoralGardens_Nahema2_Audiolog_Dialogue`; `DA_DataBankEntry_Nahema2`]

Nahema's reconstruction:

- Not a navigation error — the ship had years to correct course.
- A human phase gate would have allowed return; Zezura had no gates en route.
- **Conclusion:** the *Cicada* must have detected an *alien* phase gate, diverted to investigate, and been unable to return. [`DA_CoralGardens_Nahema2_Audiolog_Dialogue`; `DA_DataBankEntry_Nahema2`]

The orrery and starmap confirm: Proteus orbits a gas giant in a binary red-dwarf system near the galactic core. *"The Cicada mission is now effectively a separate civilization. The only possibility of rescue or return lies in whatever phenomena brought the Cicada to Proteus."* [`DA_GalacticPosition_DatabankEntry`]

Wreckage from the ship is scattered across the Coral Gardens — Empty Hold, Lander Garage, Canteen, EVA Prep, Therapy Room, Stairway to Heaven. The pattern (some compartments are from *lander modules*, but others are from the Cicada itself) suggests the ship may have broken apart violently. [`DA_POI_CicadaWreck02_DatabankEntry`; `DA_POI_CicadaWreck03_DatabankEntry`; `DA_POI_CicadaWreck04_DatabankEntry`; `DA_POI_CicadaWreckRuins01_DatabankEntry`; `DA_POI_CicadaWreckRuins02_DatabankEntry`; `DA_POI_CicadaWreckRuins03_DatabankEntry`]

## IV. The Coral Gardens Colony — A Day-by-Day Tragedy

NoA prints colonists out of **Sleep Bay 3**. [`DA_Audiolog_Exodus2_DatabankEntry`; `DA_Investigation_Singh_DatabankEntry__ClosedInvestigations`]

The first wave's habitat is **Camp One**, which fails utterly: *"Decommissioned due to high print attrition."* The original Nahema is among those who vanish; her bones are later recovered by Zip. [`DA_BaseBlackbox_CampOne`; `DA_POI_Camp_One_DatabankEntry`; `DA_CoralGardens_Nahema1_Audiolog_Dialogue`; `DA_DataBankEntry_Nahema1`]

A second wave builds **Habitat**. [`DA_BaseBlackbox_Habitat`; `DA_POI_Habitat_DatabankEntry`]

### Medical reality (Sophie's safety briefing)

- **Heavy metals everywhere** — lead, mercury, arsenic, cadmium, thallium. ~3 months to organ failure. No treatment. [`DA_BaseBlackbox_HabitatMedical`; `DA_DataBankEntry_ProteusMedicalRisks`]
- **Indigestible food** — alien amino-acid codes; causes gout and intestinal crystals. [`DA_BaseBlackbox_HabitatMedical`; `DA_Adaptations_Digestion_DatabankEntry`]
- **Pervasive Proteavirus** — every drop of seawater is loaded; should not replicate in human cells. [`DA_OceanViruses`; `DA_Proteavirus`; `DA_ProteavirusTechnicalDetails`]
- **Masefield syndrome** — delusional disorder that compels colonists to swim toward the **World Tree**. Only "cure": a hard NoA reset. [`DA_BaseBlackbox_HabitatMedical`; `DA_Audiolog_Wu_BlightLab`]

Two adaptations from milking **angel combs**: one lets you digest Protean tissue, the other gives you heat and pressure tolerance. The catch: the angel comb is a viral reservoir, which is what makes Masefield possible. [`DA_AngelComb_DatabankEntry`; `DA_Adaptations_Digestion_DatabankEntry`; `DA_Adaptations_HeatResistance_DatabankEntry`; `DA_Adaptations_BreathingAdaptation_DatabankEntry`]

### Day 400 — Nahema sees the warning sign

> *"Day 400. Bad news. I see signs of eutrophication. There are a lot of jellies here. They're feeding on decay… somewhere in this ocean, everything is dying, and it's causing runaway growth downcurrent. This is how mass extinctions get started."* [`DA_CoralGardens_Bioresearch3_Audiolog_Dialogue`; `DA_DataBankEntry_Bioresearch3`; `DA_Eutrophication`]

The biome is operating at a loss. *"Life sustained by its own rot. It's a pyramid scheme."* [`DA_CoralGardensEcology`; `DA_CoralGardens_Kurultai3_Audiolog_Dialogue`; `DA_DataBankEntry_Kurultai3`]

### Day 585 — Sophie consults the Quaker about NoA

Sophie's kurultai consultation with chaplain-psychiatrist Lucretia "Quaker" Kapoor:

> *"It has no fixed model of reality. It can entertain any madness imaginable and many more you cannot imagine, as long as those delusions help it complete the mission… The NoA is insane, but only when it's useful."* [`DA_CoralGardens_Sophie_QuakerConsult_Audiolog_Dialogue`; `DA_DataBankEntry_Sophie_QuakerConsult`]

### Day 620 — The Jubilee schism

Sophie ends medical treatment for the sick (calorie shortage). **Anita Gottschall** leads five colonists out of Habitat in protest. They name themselves **the Jubilee** — after the Babylonian custom of cancelling debts at the start of a new king's reign. They believe Proteus is trying to feed them through the angel combs. [`DA_CoralGardens_Kurultai1_Audiolog_Dialogue`; `DA_DataBankEntry_Kurultai1`; `DA_CoralGardens_SophieTriage_Audiolog_Dialogue`; `DA_DataBankEntry_SophieTriage_QuartzChip`]

Sophie's private worry: Anita is *compelled to the Tree by Masefield*, like a rat driven into a cat's jaws by parasites. [`DA_CoralGardens_Kurultai1_Audiolog_Dialogue`]

The Jubilee's recruitment broadcast: *"Are you tired of death and fear? Do you know that this world doesn't have to be our enemy? Do you feel the call of the tree?… Find us at Camp One."* [`DA_CoralGardens_JubileeMessage_Audiolog_Dialogue`; `DA_DataBankEntry_JubileeMessage_QuartzChip`]

### Day 648 — Sleep Bay 3 sabotage

Chap, in copper-poisoning vocal tics, reports the Jubilee has cut the cable from Sleep Bay 3's NoA to Habitat's NoA. They want to be printed from older biobed images that predate their Masefield infections. Sophie, quietly: *"I suspect that's what happened to the people who built Camp One."* [`DA_CoralGardens_Kurultai2_Audiolog_Dialogue`; `DA_DataBankEntry_Kurultai2`]

### Day 665 — Sophie and Nahema talk past each other

Sophie: *"How do we complete the mission if the coral gardens are dying?"*
Nahema: *"Did you think I just meant the coral gardens?"* [`DA_CoralGardens_Kurultai3_Audiolog_Dialogue`; `DA_DataBankEntry_Kurultai3`]

### Day 670 — Sophie's secret therapy

Patient chart, *Boucher, S.*: Sophie has been secretly recycling herself daily through a biobed to delay her own Masefield progress. *"Did not tell Nahema, best not to. Diverging perspectives on viability of mission."* [`DA_Sophie_SelfTreatment`]

### Zip dies and is reset (~Day 745)

Zip drowns in the Hot Cave. NoA refuses to reprint her from her current image (too contaminated) and prints her from her boarding-day biobed scan instead. **The new Zip remembers nothing of her friends.** [`DA_CoralGardens_BlackBox_Zip_01_DatabankEntry`; `DA_Ruins_BlackBox_Zip_02_DatabankEntry`; `DA_CoralGardens_Jubilee2_Audiolog_Dialogue`; `DA_DataBankEntry_Jubilee2_QuartzChip`; `DA_Investigation_ZipBlackbox_DatabankEntry`]

The "reset Zip" is later seen by Nahema not recognizing their old friendship — *"So… before I got reset… we were friends?" "Yeah. We were." "Is it okay if I write that down?"* [`DA_Audiolog_KarakorumPower3MethaneTanks_DatabankEntry`; `DA_CoralGardens_Nahema3_Audiolog_Dialogue`-adjacent]

Anita's reaction in *"Zip is dead"*:
> *"Sleep Bay 3 printed a new copy of Tziporah Malk. She was not our Zip… Chap says NoA 'reset her' to her 'last known good state.' I am aghast… We are slaves."* [`DA_CoralGardens_Jubilee2_Audiolog_Dialogue`; `DA_DataBankEntry_Jubilee2_QuartzChip`]

### Day 755 — Wu cracks the bloom strain

> *"I think I've cracked it. Sophie and Anita are BOTH right. The Proteavirus causes Masefield syndrome… but only one kind of Proteavirus. The bloom strain. Maybe it's tainting Anita's angel comb adaptations. I've tuned the sonic resonators to destroy the viral crystals grown by the bloom strain."* [`DA_CoralGardens_BlightLab_Wu_Resonator_Audiolog_Dialogue`; `DA_Audiolog_Wu_BlightLab`; `DA_Investigation_WuBlackbox_DatabankEntry`]

### Day 758 — The Hot Cave Incident

I can hear the whole planet…"* [`DA_CoralGardens_CavePitch_Audiolog_Dialogue`; `DA_DeepCaveRoots`; `DA_Ch2_DeepCave_Zip_WeCanLiveHere_DatabankEntry`]

A group descends to recover Zip's old body (and its kurul key). The hot water triggers Masefield. **Ganzo gives his oxygen mask to a dead crab.** Twinkle — the one un-adapted member of the group, kept behind on most outings — drops her tanks and reprints to safety. [`DA_CoralGardens_Twinkle_Response_Audiolog_Dialogue`; `DA_Dialogue_Story_Ch2_CGG_Blackbox_Ganz`; `DA_Hint_DeepCaveKurulKey`]
